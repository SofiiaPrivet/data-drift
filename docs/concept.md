1. CORE THESIS
Humans continuously adapt to invisible environmental conditions.
Over time, exposure does not produce visible collapse, but gradual desensitization.
This project explores how a system slowly reduces its sensitivity in response to environmental
stress.
The viewer is placed inside a responsive atmospheric organism driven by real-time urban air data.
The tragedy is not destruction.
The tragedy is normalization.

2. CONCEPTUAL FRAME
Urban air pollution is treated not as a crisis event but as a permanent condition of
metropolitan life.
The organism does not resist pollution.
It adapts.
Responsiveness slowly decreases as exposure accumulates.
When pollution decreases, recovery occurs — but gradually.
The system never fully loses sensitivity.
A minimal irreducible response always remains.

3. SYSTEM LOGIC
Data is translated through a behavioral model:
Data → state → behavior → perception
Three continuous processes operate simultaneously:
Baseline respiration
Constant atmospheric movement representing vitality.
Immediate pollution response
Real-time pollution levels modify the organism’s behavior.
Adaptive memory accumulation
Pollution exposure accumulates over time and gradually reduces system sensitivity.
Adaptation operates on a longer temporal scale than pollution fluctuation.
Sensitivity decreases but never reaches zero.

4. BEHAVIORAL MAPPING
Each pollutant affects a different behavioral layer of the organism:
NO₂ → membrane tension
PM2.5 → particle density
PM10 → vibration field
CO → light temperature
Pollution levels modify these behaviors in real time.
Accumulated exposure gradually dampens the amplitude and speed of all responses.
Adaptation should operate on a longer time scale than pollution fluctuation.

5. SPATIAL STRUCTURE
The chamber functions as an atmospheric organism.
It consists of:
• a responsive membrane
• suspended particle fields
• volumetric light conditions
• subtle vibration dynamics
Baseline respiration persists independently of pollution.
Pollution modifies the breath.

6. EMOTIONAL TONE
Two atmospheric conditions exist:
Low pollution
• subtle breathing
• open spatial feeling
• calm but alive
• slight shimmer
High pollution
• increased membrane tension
• heavier particles
• colder light
• denser atmosphere
As adaptation accumulates, reactions become slower and softer.
The system stabilizes.
This stability may feel calming, but it indicates reduced sensitivity.

7. TEMPORAL EXPERIENCE
The viewer does not observe distinct phases.
Instead, they inhabit a continuously evolving atmospheric condition.
Pollution fluctuates rapidly.
Adaptation evolves slowly.
The experience is systemic rather than narrative.

8. VIEWER POSITION
The viewer is located inside the organism.
There are no data interfaces or numerical displays.
Only spatial behavior communicates the system state.
Interaction is limited to camera movement and perspective change.

9. AUDIENCE
Creative technology studios
Interactive installation agencies
Digital art institutions
Exhibition design studios
The project demonstrates spatial data translation and behavioral system design.

10. EXPERIENCE DURATION
Focused viewing: approximately 2–3 minutes.
Installation mode: continuous operation.
Within several minutes, viewers may begin to perceive subtle dampening of responsiveness.

12. FAILURE CONDITIONS
The project fails if it becomes:
• a sci-fi energy core
• generic WebGL organic animation
• visually spectacular but conceptually empty
• overly explanatory
Restraint and subtlety are essential.

13. EXIT EMOTION
The viewer leaves with a quiet question:
Is the system stable — or has it simply stopped responding?
A subtle awareness of environmental dependence emerges.
