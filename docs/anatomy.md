2. ORGANISM MEMBRANE
Semi-Visible Membrane
• lung tissue
• cell membrane
• organism boundary

Properties:
semi-transparent
soft
elastic
slow movement

Material direction:
• subsurface scattering feel
• faint internal glow
• visible thickness at edges
• tension wrinkles mixed

Motion behavior:
breathing cycle
slow expansion
 slow contraction
Baseline rhythm:
~8–12 second cycle
High NO₂ →
breath becomes shallow
membrane tension increases
clean air → slight wrinkles, no tension
pollution ↑ → wrinkles clusters appear, tension increse 
pollution high → deep wrinkles, heavy movement 
adaptation stage → wrinkles freeze / lose motion

The membrane is never completely still. Its movement creates natural patterns. The level of pollution regulates the tension, haviness and intensity of the pattern. The breath can remain in the membrane zones, but not the whole membrane.

3. PARTICLE ATMOSPHERE
two particle layers which form clusters (islands)
Layer A (⚈) — Micro particles (PM2.5) small
Behavior:
slow drifting
Brownian-like movement
almost dust-like
Visual feel: like light floating dust in air
Layer B (✗) — Macro particles (PM10) Larger
Behavior:
slower
heavier
occasionally crossing light beams
They must remain rare.
PM2.5 max ≈ 6k–8k particles
PM10 max ≈ 800–1200 particles
⎥Clusters can be created using noise fields or attractor points.

4. VOLUMETRIC LIGHTING
Primary light source
Soft directional beam.
Like light entering through a biological opening.
Color baseline:
warm neutral
Pollution influence
Pollution shifts light temperature:
clean air → warmer
polluted air → colder / greenish
Very subtle.Medical light
Volumetric rays
Visible only when particles cross them.
This creates moments where pollution suddenly becomes perceptible.
Key rule:
light should reveal particles
not dominate the scene

5. TEMPORAL RHYTHM

The entire system should feel like breathing biology.
Baseline motion speeds:
membrane breathing: 7–11 sec
particle drift: continuous slow ( increse with pollution)
light shift: extremely slow
Adaptation system will gradually reduce response amplitude.
Meaning:
The organism becomes quieter over time.
